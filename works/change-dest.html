Introduction

Change destination is a feature that enables users to change where they are going when their ride has already started and the driver has accepted the ride request.
In this presentation, we are going through the process of coming up with the idea, noticing the problem and finding a solution for it.

My Role

Designer

Team

Ramin Fazli (Product Manager), Iman Bagherpour (iOS Developer), Meysam Shohrat (Android Developer), and me

Problem Statement

Everyday ~2K of the calls to our Call Center (CC) were about users wanting to change their destination. The number of calls informed us about this problem and we decided to run a survey.

We ran the survey and from the reported results, we found out that 14% of the rides requested destination change mid-trip. 30% of them used one of our Ride Options which is adding a Second Destination, 26% asked the driver and negotiated a price, 20% did nothing and finished their ride, 9% asked the drivers but they didn’t accept to change their destination, and 7% called CC.

Solution

To answer the users’ needs, we came up with the Change Destination feature in Passenger application. We started by benchmarking international ride hailing applications and drawing the flow and specified the touch points.

Wireframes

After finishing the flow and having an overall understanding of what the vision was, I started wire-framing.  

Final design

After a lot of iterations, the designs were final and it was time to have a demo session for our development team. After the session I modified some of the designs and we had them ready to be implemented.

Final design in flow

Results

We ran a survey again after releasing Change Destination to check both accessibility and usability. The results were positive and it scored a total of 91% for the accessibility and usability, and 79% for driver awareness of this feature.
Also we checked our CC calls regarding change destination requests and it has reduced the calls.
