Introduction

Missions were added to Snapp to reduce the cost of retention campaigns, enhance the engagement, and increase the usage of our product.

My Role

Designer, Illustrator

Team

Ramin Fazli (Product Manager), Iman Bagherpour (iOS Developer), Akram Ghaseminia (Android Developer), and me

Problem Statement

In the Coronavirus pandemic, our ride requests plummeted down so much that we couldn't give away the normal vouchers like we used to because of the cost for the company. Aside from the cost, they wouldn't bring loyalty and engagement from our users, so we had to make more efficient vouchers.

Solution

We came up with three types of vouchers with gamification.


  • Target Based Vouchers
    A new type of conditional voucher that passenger must take rides with some conditions like service type, deadline time, etc.After meeting the target, reward voucher code will be unlocked for the passenger and they can start using the code.

  • Direct Discount Vouchers
    It's an alternative for cash back functionality that passenger redeem a code and get the reward discount in their next ride directly.

  • Compound Vouchers
    A promotion code with different discount values base on the time of code redemption.

Final Designs

For the designs, since we had vouchers before, I tried to keep it as simple and similar to that of previous designs so it would seem familiar to the users.

Results

Based on our A/B tests, these mission type vouchers has reduced the cost of the normal vouchers, and it has increased conversion rate.

Also, they brought back the daily rides count to its value before the pandemic and increased retention.

We ran a survey after releasing these vouchers the results showed that a good number of users copy the voucher code from their SMS messages. To fix this issue we’re planning to bring the missions in our Loyalty Club, and place a button in the checkout funnel to show active voucher codes from there.
